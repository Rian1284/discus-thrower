The Theory

Two Icons. One Body.

Myron's Discobolus (450 BC)

Art historian Kenneth Clark identified two defining qualities in Myron's sculpture: rhythmos — harmony and flow — and symmetria — perfect bodily proportion. The Discobolus is not the most muscular figure in antiquity; he is the most balanced. Every muscle contributes to a whole. The Greeks called this ideal kalokagathia — that physical and moral excellence are inseparable. The Discobolus gave birth to the Adonis Index: the shoulder circumference should be 1.618× the waist. This is your structural north star.

Toji Fushiguro (JJK, Modern)

Toji's physique is the Discobolus rendered in 2D ink and shading — same proportions, more explicit muscularity. The crucial analysis: it's the V-taper above everything else. Wide lats, capped medial delts, a narrow waist, thick upper back (traps, rhomboids, rear delts), and arms that are visible, veined, and dense without being bodybuilder-bloated. His body is built for motion — not for a front-facing mirror pose. He looks powerful from every angle. That requires the muscles most gym-goers ignore: the full posterior shoulder, the upper traps, the serratus.

Version 2.0

What Has Been Upgraded

⚠

Critical Gap in V1

The original programme was excellent for chest and lat width but under-served the medial and posterior deltoids — the two muscle groups most responsible for the Toji silhouette. Lateral raises were in the plan but at too low a volume (4×15–20 once a week). Evidence indicates the lateral deltoid needs 10–20 sets per week across multiple sessions for meaningful hypertrophy. This has been corrected. Rear delts and upper traps were entirely absent. They are now present on every single training day.

Medial deltoid — metabolic stress, hypertrophy cap

Chest, triceps — loaded pressing

 3 × 12–15 From push-up top position: push further forward, rounding upper back, pushing shoulder blades apart. Hold 2s. Return. This makes the fingers of muscle beside the ribs visible when lean.

Pull

⏱ 55–70 min 💪 9 exercises + finisher
Exercise Sets × Reps Execution Cue
KeyPull-up (overhand, wide grip)
Lats, upper back, biceps — lat width
 5 × max Full dead hang at bottom — complete stretch. Chest to bar at top. 3s down. Wide grip = more lat, narrower = more bicep. Prioritize wide for V-taper.
KeyChin-up (underhand)
Biceps, lower lats — arm density
 4 × max Supinate hard at top. Dead hang at bottom. Bicep peak is built here.
Negative Pull-up (5s descent)
Eccentric overload — builds pull-up count fast
 3 × 5–6 Jump to top position. Lower in exactly 5 seconds. No cheating. This is your eccentric lat and bicep stimulus.
Inverted Row
Mid-back, rhomboids, posterior delts
 4 × 12–15 Body straight as a plank. Pull chest to bar. At the top: squeeze shoulder blades together and hold 1s. This activates rhomboids properly.
DBDB Single-Arm Row
Lat thickness, mid-back — mass builder
 4 × 10–12 ea. Brace on bed. Full stretch at bottom, drive elbow to hip — not elbow to ceiling. 3s down. This is your primary lat thickness movement.
NewDBDB Rear Delt Fly (bent over)
Posterior deltoid — the missing muscle for 3D shoulder aesthetics
 4 × 15–20 Hinge 45° forward. Slight bend in elbows — do NOT straighten them. Lead with elbows, raise to shoulder height. Pinch shoulder blades at top. Rear delts are a small muscle — don't ego-lift. Use both 5kg dumbbells. Slow and controlled.
NewDBDB Shrug
Upper trapezius — neck-to-shoulder column
 4 × 15–20 Hold both dumbbells. Shrug straight UP — not forward or backward. Hold 1s at top. Full stretch at bottom. Traps respond to moderate-high reps. This builds the neck-trap silhouette that makes a V-taper look imposing from the front.
DBDB Bicep Curl
Bicep isolation — arm density and definition
 3 × 12–15 Alternating. Full supination at top. 3s negative. No swinging. Squeeze and twist hard at peak.
Scapular Pull-up + Dead Hang
Shoulder health, scapula stability, grip
 3 × 10 + 30s 10 scap pulls then hold dead hang for 30s without break. Builds the foundation for injury-free long-term pulling.

Every Day

Greasing the Groove — Non-Negotiable

🔔

Every time you walk past the bar — 1–2 pull-ups or a 20s dead hang

This is unchanged from V1 and it stays because it works. Never to failure — stay at 50% of your max. 8–10 times per day, this adds massive pull-up volume without touching recovery. This is how people go from 3 pull-ups to 15 in 3 months.

V2 addition: On non-training days, optionally add 1×10 lateral raises with a very light weight every time you do a GTG pull-up set. Sub-threshold, never to failure. This extends your lateral delt frequency without adding recovery cost — the side delts are a small, fast-recovering muscle. Even 3–4 extra sub-maximal sets per day on rest days accumulates meaningful additional weekly volume.

Why Compression Shirts Work A compression shirt compresses the waist and chest slightly, but the shoulders and arms push outward against the fabric. The visual result is a more pronounced V-taper. The muscles that show through compression fabric: medial deltoids (shoulder caps), bicep/tricep mass, upper chest fullness, and the neck-trap column. Every upgrade in this V2 programme directly targets these areas.

The Toji Leanness Note Toji's body fat is estimated at 8–10%. The Discobolus shows every muscle without exaggeration. At 65 kg and 175 cm, you are already in a good range for body composition. The goal is not to lose weight — it's to maintain weight while adding muscle to the right places (shoulders, lats, traps) and staying lean. Rice is not your enemy. Protein and consistency are your allies. The visible definition will come from progressive muscle gain + maintained leanness, not from starvation.
The One New Rule for V2 On training days, add one extra protein source post-workout within 45 minutes. This doesn't need to be a shake — one extra egg, an extra piece of fish, or a glass of milk does the job. The increased volume in this programme creates more muscle protein synthesis demand. Feed it.
